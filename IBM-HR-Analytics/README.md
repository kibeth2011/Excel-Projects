# HR Analytics Dashboard in Excel
## Project Overview
This project analyzes employee attrition and workforce trends using the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance dataset. The objective was to identify the key factors influencing employee turnover, understand workforce demographics, evaluate employee characteristics, and provide actionable insights to support HR decision-making.

Using Microsoft Excel, the project combines PivotTables, PivotCharts, KPI cards, slicers, and an interactive dashboard to transform raw HR data into meaningful business insights.

## Dataset
- <a href="https://https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/pavansubhasht/ibm-hr-analytics-attrition-dataset?resource=download">IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance Dataset</a>

The dataset contains 1,470 employee records with information on:
- Employee demographics
- Department
- Job Role
- Monthly Income
- Education Field
- Business Travel
- Overtime
- Job Satisfaction
- Attrition
- Years at Company

## A. Workforce Overview
### 1. How many employees are in the company?
The organization employs 1,470 people, providing a substantial workforce for analyzing employee demographics and attrition trends.

### 2. What is the overall attrition rate?
The overall attrition rate is 16.12%. It means that approximately 16% of employees left the company, indicating opportunities to improve employee retention.

### 3. What is the average employee age?
The average employee age is 37 years implying that the workforce is relatively young.

### 4. What is the average monthly income?
Employees earn an average monthly income of $6,502.93, providing a benchmark for compensation analysis.

### 5. What is the average number of years employees stay at the company?
Employees remain with the company for an average of 7.01 years, suggesting a moderate workforce stability.

# Key Findings
1. The company employs 1,470 employees, with an overall attrition rate of 16.12%.
2. Employees remain with the company for an average of 7.01 years.
3. The Research & Development department recorded the highest employee attrition.
4. Laboratory Technicians experienced the highest employee turnover.
5. Employees working overtime were considerably more likely to leave the company.
6. Employees who travel frequently recorded the highest attrition rate among business travel categories.
7. Employees with a Human Resources education field experienced the highest attrition.
8. The workforce is predominantly male (60%), with employees aged 26–35 years forming the largest age group.

# Recommendations
1. Investigate the causes of high attrition within the Research & Development department and develop targeted retention strategies.
2. Review workload distribution and overtime practices to reduce employee burnout and improve retention.
3. Evaluate travel policies for employees who travel frequently and explore flexible work arrangements where appropriate.
4. Conduct role-specific retention initiatives for Laboratory Technicians, including career development and employee engagement programs.
5. Monitor attrition trends by education field to identify workforce segments that may require additional support.
6. Continue monitoring workforce demographics to support succession planning and long-term talent management.
